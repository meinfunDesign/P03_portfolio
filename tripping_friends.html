Tripping Friends

Art Direction / Character Design

Overview

Tripping Friends is a third person novel game targeted to teenagers and young adults are interested in adventurous novel style game with a slight of violence action such as blood, explosion, and so on.The story follows Birdy, who wakes up in a strange forest. He has no recollection of how he ended up here, but he feels familiarity and discomfort.

My role in this project mainly focused on concept ideation, character sketch and visual development design on the project’s form and features, as well as storyboard and flowchart for game stages. I also held smaller roles in writing the script and selecting background music in the final month to increase the immersive experience during the gameplay.

Team

Sherry Liao, Lexi Feng, Jessica Hsu, Doris Huang, Brandon Kanegae Tse.

Context

This is a three-months project for a senior Narrative and New Media course.

Tools

Illustrator, Photoshop

Deliverables

Interactive Game link

Process

Art Direction

The art direction of this project is inspired by the animated video series of violent adventure and narrative - Happy Tree Friends. The series includes cute woodland creatures that go through a happy adventure which turns into extreme graphic violence that causes injuries and deaths.

Storyline

The story begins with Birdy is lost in the strange forest. He is determined to find out what had happened to him. Birdy ventures through different areas and finds himself in strange places while meeting even stranger creatures. Birdy is left to make difficult choices which may lead him to find out about the hidden truths of this strange world. However, after everything that happens he may wish he had never arrived here at all.

Flowchart

3 stages and twist ending

  • Stage 1: In the beginning, the player as the main character Birdy wakes up in the Dark Forest, he can either choose to follow Flipsy to go to the Mushroom House or go alone.
  • Stage 2: He meets Tinko at Mushroom House, and eventually gets the clue to go to the castle.
  • Stage 3: Player meets Ollie outside the castle and decides to go to follow the map to go to Dungeon. In the castle, he is forced to choose between 2 doors by Sanny. One of the doors might reveal the truth Birdy is seeking to explore.

Reflection

What I found to the biggest takeaway:


being design-driven with a concept in mind.

When our team came up with the idea of “an enthralling storyline with several twists presented in a violent yet cartoonish style,” it changed my view of what a project is. The project is not only can evoke people's curiosity to choose the story direction during the gameplay, but also the ending can be reflective for people to rethink what humanity is. I seek to bring this design-driven approach to future projects.

What I would Improve:


the experience between the visual animation and game atmosphere.

Our team came up a well-structured the storyline and portfolio quality, but we failed to find different soundtracks to fit different scenes in the game: the immersive feeling of being surrounded by Dark Forest, Mushroom House and Dungeon, the sound effect of character movements, and the ambience of realistic immersion to accompany it all. Incorporating this into our final product would give the player high-quality appeal to have better game experience in the game.
